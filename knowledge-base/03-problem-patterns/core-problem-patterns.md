title: strategy_execution_gap
category: problem-patterns
trigger: why is strategy not translating into execution; execution gaps; why things break down after planning
short_answer: Execution gaps usually happen when alignment is weaker in practice than it looks in meetings, decks, or plans.
approved_answer: The gap between strategy and execution usually appears when priorities are interpreted differently across the organization, tradeoffs are not clear, ownership is blurred, or leaders are not getting the unfiltered reality back quickly enough. The result is that the strategy may sound agreed, but the actual operating behavior underneath it is inconsistent.
expand_if_asked: This is one reason leadership teams can feel confident about direction while still seeing delays, rework, uneven follow-through, or decisions that keep getting escalated.

title: recurring_org_problems_after_interventions
category: problem-patterns
trigger: why do the same issues keep coming back; why interventions do not stick; why problems reappear
short_answer: Problems often keep coming back because the visible symptom gets addressed, but the deeper system pattern does not.
approved_answer: When organizational issues keep reappearing, it is usually because the response treated the symptom rather than the structure creating it. A team may run a workshop, change a process, or replace a leader, but if the deeper friction is still there, the pattern tends to return in a slightly different form.
expand_if_asked: This does not mean interventions never work. It means leaders often need a clearer diagnosis of what is driving the pattern before they decide what kind of action is actually appropriate.
