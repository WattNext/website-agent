## Answer Unit

```md
title: trust_vs_psychological_safety
category: proof-and-trust
trigger: trust vs psychological safety; what is the difference between trust and psychological safety
short_answer: Trust and psychological safety are related, but they are not identical. A team can feel broadly positive and still avoid speaking with full candor when the stakes are high.
approved_answer: Trust is often about confidence in people, intent, or relationships. Psychological safety is more about whether people believe they can speak honestly, raise risk, or disagree without harmful consequences. That distinction matters because an organization can look healthy at a surface level and still fail to surface critical truth when leadership most needs it.
expand_if_asked: This helps explain why broad positive sentiment does not always translate into useful signal for decision-making.
```
